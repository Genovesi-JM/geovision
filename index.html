MODULES

Core building blocks of the project.

Each module can be developed step by step, depending on the budget and use case.

Drone & LiDAR Mapping

Plan flights, capture imagery and generate orthomosaics, elevation models and detailed terrain profiles for any field or concession.

Soil & Water Sensing

Deploy IoT probes to monitor moisture, pH, NPK, EC and temperature, then see the readings visualised on the map as zones and trends.

Analytics & Indices

Convert raw data into NDVI-style vegetation indices, risk scores or performance grades, so decision-makers get a clear “red / amber / green” view.

Reports & Exports

Export maps, statistics and field notes as PDFs, images or slide-ready visuals to share with clients, management or authorities.

SECTORS

Where GeoVision can be applied.

AGRICULTURE

Precision agriculture & irrigation

Identify stressed zones, drainage problems and variability inside each field. Combine drone maps with moisture sensors to plan irrigation and input use more efficiently.

Better yields and fewer blind spots on the farm.

MINING & QUARRIES

Volume, stability & safety

Map pits, stockpiles and haul roads, track changes over time and flag high-risk areas such as unstable slopes, restricted zones or informal access paths.

A clearer picture of the site, updated regularly.

INFRASTRUCTURE & RISK

Corridors & danger zones

Monitor roads, power lines, pipelines or other linear assets and map nearby risk areas – from erosion and flooding to suspected hazardous zones.

Better planning and prioritisation for field teams.

WORKFLOW

How a GeoVision project runs.

A typical engagement can be structured in four clear phases.

1

Plan

Define the area of interest, objectives, data sources (drones, sensors, existing maps) and how often the site needs to be updated.

2

Capture

Execute drone flights, collect LiDAR or imagery and retrieve sensor data from the field following safe, standardised procedures.

3

Process

Process the data into maps, models and indices using GIS/photogrammetry tools, then load them into the GeoVision dashboard.

4

Deliver & act

Share dashboards, maps and reports with decision-makers, and define concrete actions for each high-priority zone on the map.
